Research Interests

I am interested in language documentation and revitalization.

My research has focused on Mi'gmaq, a First Nations Eastern Algonquian language. I am part of a larger group collaboration with Mi'gmaq speakers and language teachers in the Mi'gmaq Listuguj community (located at the border of Quebec and New Brunswick). The collaboration is a collective effort to develop tools for language teaching, documentation, and analysis. You can learn more about the ongoing project here and read some news from last summer here. (On the right: the view of Listuguj across the river. Photo taken from Sugarloaf Mountain in Campbellton, NB.)

On the theoretical side I am interested in (morpho)syntax. My thesis investigated noun incorporation and compounding. I'm also a fan of evaluative morphology (diminutives, augmentatives) cross-linguistically.
